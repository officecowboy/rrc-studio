When is a cloud no longer a cloud?

The model loading behind this text could have began in any number of digital formats, but it is now something different entirely. Like a cloud formed of millions of droplets, sample images have been distributed across the neurons of a Neural Radiance Field (NeRF) and precomputed into a representational form capable of synthesizing novel, three-dimensional views in real time.

This rendering technology, a Sparse Neural Radiance Grid (SNeRG), offers a glimpse into the potential of artificial intelligence to reshape reality as we know it. Just as the clouds above us morph into new shapes before our eyes, so too does the media we use to represent them. Learn more about the SNeRG from the original paper by Google Research.

The Reality Recycling Center (RRC) creates mixed reality experiences, tools, and souvenirs that spark new connections across media and technology of the past, present, and future.

Born from a series of audiovisual experiments, RRC has evolved into a multi-disciplinary creative studio with clients and collaborators in art, music, and technology.

Our team is currently pursuing graduate study in lighting design at Parsons School of Design and artificial intelligence at Stanford University. We bring a combined background and interest in computer science, audio/video production, and media studies to our work at RRC.
